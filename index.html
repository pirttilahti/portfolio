About

The first question I often ask is: "Who are we designing for?"

I'm one the few people with a degree in HTI (Human-Technology Interaction). On top of which I have another degree in human behavior (BSc, Psychology). But the "black on white" isn't what sets me apart from all the exceptional talent in the field. What really separates me is the way I've learned to think about technological challenges with the combination of years of theoretical knowledge and hands-on experience.

To me design isn't about learning the best practices in the industry, it isn't about making things pretty, nor is it about making things as simple as possible. To me design is understanding the humans (users) that will be in contact with what we want to build and solving the challenges facing them.

Too often designers design for themselves or those closest to themselves. I want to design for those that will actually use the solution. I want to create lasting value and experiences that make peoples lives better.

I understand both the business side and the users side and believe, a carefully balanced mix of these is what truly creates winning products.
